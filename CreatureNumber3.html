The Spiky-Nosed Ghost Shark (*Hydrolagus erithacus*) was first discovered in 2016 off the coasts of South Africa and Madagascar. This rare deep-sea species is part of the chimaera family, a group of cartilaginous fish closely related to sharks and rays. It is distinguished by its long, spiky snout and smooth, ghostly body, which earned it its eerie nickname. Scientists discovered the species during deep-sea trawling expeditions aimed at exploring the largely unknown biodiversity of the ocean floor. Although its existence was noted in 2016, it was formally described and named in 2020, underscoring the challenges of studying deep-sea creatures.
